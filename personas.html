Personas and Storyboards

Project Overview

Do you remember the first time you used a microwave? Personally, I don't, but I do remember the first time I used the new microwave on the 4th floor of the CIT. I was confused and could not figure out how to open it when my food was done heating.

This confusion inspired me to figure out if other people had a similar experience and how I could change that. I then decided to observe and survey 3 people to learn about their experiences using the microwave. After collecting the survey results and observations, I created 2 personas to represent different types of users that may interact with the CIT microwave. Finally, I created a storyboard to illustrate the end-to-end experience of using the microwave.

This entire process helped me understand the needs, wants and feelings of the microwave users and how to change the design to potentially improve user experience.

Survey Details

I first surveyed 3 people who were using the microwave in the 4th floor of the CIT. I then collected their responses and observed their interactions with the microwave. Here are the results of the survey and observations:

Observations

  • All interviewees used the microwave to heat food
  • Each interviewee used a different button to set the time to heat food
    • One interviewee manually set the cooking time by using the number pad
    • A second interviewee pressed “Quick 30” 3 times to cook their food for 1.5 minutes
    • A third interviewee pressed the “Popcorn” button to heat up some popcorn
  • All interviewees struggled to open the door after heating food due to the child-lock feature
    • One interviewee thought they broke the microwave when it wouldn't open
  • Two interviewees mistakenly used the “Timer” instead of “Add Time” button.
    • “Add Time” sets the time you want the food to cook, and “Timer” creates a countdown clock that does not activate the microwave.

Survey Results

  1. What do you use the microwave for?

    Response: All interviewees use the microwave to heat up food.

  2. How often do you use the microwave?

    Response: Two of the interviewees use the microwave multiple times a week. One interviewee uses the microwave a few times a month.

  3. Is the microwave easier or harder to use than other microwaves? Why?

    Response: All interviewees reported that the microwave is harder to use than other microwaves because of the auto-lock feature. They found the microwave unintuitive because you had to press 0 after heating the food to unlock the microwave.

  4. Did you have any trouble with the microwave the first time you used it?

    Response: The first time the interviewees used the microwave, they struggled to open the door and had to figure out how to unlock it. They also were confused by the “Timer” and “Add Time” buttons.

  5. Would you change anything about the microwave?

    Response: Interviewees said they would remove the auto-lock feature and make the instructions for unlocking the microwave more obvious.

Conclusion

My main takeaway from this project is the importance of prioritizing the user when designing products, especially AI applications. Creating personas and storyboards taught me how to gage the needs, wants, and feelings of stakeholders. Getting feedback from real users showed me how much people value a simple, easy-to-use interface. Moreover, I learned that you should never assume a product, even a common one, will be intuitive without first testing with actual users. The skills I've gained from this project will be essential in designing user-friendly and accessible AI applications in the future.
