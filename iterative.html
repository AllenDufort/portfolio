Project Overview

"Sesame: Iterative Design" was a group project for the course CSCI 1300: Interaction Design. The goal of the project was to create a design website for a startup company using an iterative design process. I, together with Sofia Guarisma, Kaley Newlin, and Valentina Lin, created a new website design for Sesame, a startup that strives to provide affordable and accessible healthcare to all.

Sketches and Wireframe

Our design process began with individual sketches to tackle three key goals: creating personalized and engaging webpages, simplifying medical information, and ensuring intuitive navigation.

Each team member's sketch brought unique ideas:

  • Allen: Welcome Page linking to other pages and page with health tips
  • Kaley: Welcome Page's layout structure and suggested action prompts
  • Sofia: Educational modules with health tips and page with health stats
  • Valentina: Layout structure of pages to research symptoms and to explain medications

Part 2: Critiques and Changes

We received valuable feedback from Vanessa Cho (VP, Google Design Platforms), Ravi Mehta (Sesame CPO), and Chris Chon (Lead Product Designer), which helped us refine our design.

Some of the main feedback was:

  • Improve visual hierarchy for better navigation
  • Remove unnecessary or confusing components
  • Clarify the purpose of each page
  • Make the flow of the Symptoms Page clearer

Conclusion

Future Changes

In the future, some changes I would like to make include:

  • Making scale of web components more standard
  • Avoiding unclear language
  • Breaking Symptoms Page into multiple single-question windows

Takeaways

Through this project, I deepened my understanding of how to design user-friendly, accessible websites, particularly those integrating AI-driven features. I learned how to redesign quickly based on feedback and make applications easier for users to navigate. This experience also strengthened my ability to merge diverse ideas into a cohesive design. All these skills, especially including different perspectives, are important when designing AI-based technology because we must ensure that the technology is accessible to everyone.
