DraftBears Sportsbook

Overview

Project Description

This project analyzed NBA player performance metrics to uncover trends and correlations that can improve sports analytics. By examining data from different times of the season, we provide insights into how player performance impacts team success.

Context

This project was created by Melvin He, José Guallpa, and I as a final project for the course CSCI 1951A: Data Science at Brown University in Spring 2024. We were inspired to do this project because we were all fans of the NBA and we wanted to learn how to use data science in sports analytics.

Research Process

A major challenge was trying to find a dataset that included both player performance metrics and team information. My team and I spent a lot of time finding datasets that had one or both of these features. We ultimately collected 2 datasets from multiple APIs and then combined them into one database that included all of the player data we needed. Our database includes various player performance indicators such as points per game, field goal percentage (FGP), defensive rebounds, and personal fouls.

Findings

1. Players with higher plus-minus scores tend to have higher FGP

  • Plus-minus score: difference between points scored by player's team and opposing team while the player on court

2. No significant difference in players' FGP in March vs entire season

3. Players with higher defensive rebounds tend to have higher personal fouls

Conclusion

Takeaways

This project deepened my understanding of how complex and nuanced data analysis can be. For example, the correlation between plus-minus scores and field goal percentage was lower than expected, revealing that many other factors — such as steals and offensive rebounds — influence player performance metrics. I also learned that even with straightforward datasets, it’s important to avoid overgeneralizing trends. Overall, this project reinforced the importance of mindful and cautious analysis, as I am to build AI and ML models that accurately reflect real-world data.

Ethical Considerations

Another key takeaway was the importance of considering ethical implications in data analysis. In sports analytics, racial bias remains a serious concern; for example, research by Foy and Roy (2023) highlighted how lighter-skinned players are often praised for cognitive abilities, while darker-skinned players are described more by physical traits. Although our dataset lacked qualitative descriptions, we took care to mitigate bias by avoiding analysis that linked physical attributes to race. As I continue working with AI and ML, maintaining ethical standards will be crucial to creating fair, inclusive, and trustworthy systems.
