DraftBears Sportsbook:

A data analysis project on NBA player performance metrics.

Overview

This project analyzed NBA player performance metrics to uncover trends and correlations that can improve sports analytics. By examining data from different times of the season, we aim to provide insights into how player performance impacts team success.

Context

This project was created by Allen Dufort, Melvin He, and Jos√© Guallpa as a final project for the course CS 1951A: Data Science at Brown University in Spring 2024. We were inspired because we are all fans of the NBA and wanted to explore the statistics behind the game. Additionally, we wanted to learn how to use data science in sports analytics.

Research Process

A major challenge was trying to find a dataset that included both player performance metrics and team information. My team and I spent a lot of time finding datasets that had one or both of these features. We ultimately collected 2 datasets from multiple APIs and then combined them into one database that included all of the player data we needed. Our database includes various player performance indicators such as points per game, field goal percentage (FGP), defensive rebounds, and personal fouls.

Findings

  1. Players with higher plus-minus scores tend to have a higher FGP.
    • Plus-minus score is difference between points scored by team and points scored by opposing team while player is on court
  2. There is no significant difference in the FGP of players in March compared to their season average.
  3. There is a correlation between the number of defensive rebounds and personal fouls committed by players.

Takeaways

One takeaway from this project is that there are many factors that affect player performance metrics. For example, the correlation between plus-minus scores and field goal percentage is lower than expected (0.179). This indicates that there are other factors that affect player performance metrics, such as steals and offensive rebounds. Another takeaway is that even in the simplistic data analysis, one should be careful about overgeneralizing the trends found in the data.

Ethical Considerations

The last takeaway is to always consider the ethical implications of the data analysis. For example, racial bias is a major issue in sports analytics. Research from Foy and Roy (2023) showed that lighter-skinned basketball players were more likely to be described for their performance and mental abilities, while darker-skinned players were more likely to be described for their physical abilities. Although the datasets we collected did not include qualitative descriptions, we still tried to mitigate bias by ensuring that our analysis did not focus on the relationship between physical attributes and race.
