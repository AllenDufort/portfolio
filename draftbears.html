DraftBears Sportsbook

Overview

Project Description

Draftbook Sportbooks is a project that analyzed NBA player performance metrics. This project, created by Melvin He, Jos√© Guallpa, and me, was a final project for CSCI 1951A: Data Science at Brown University in Spring 2024. Motivated by our shared interests in the NBA and sports analytics, we examined data from various points in the season to provide insights into how individual player performance impacts their teams' success.

Research Process

Our research question was: "How do player performance metrics impact team success?" We then broke this question down into 3 hypotheses that we could test:

  1. The higher the plus-minus score, the better the player performs in terms of scoring efficiency (higher field goal (FG)%)
  2. There is no significant difference in the FG% of players in March compared to their average FG% over the season
  3. There is no significant correlation between the number of DRBs and PFs committed by players.

One challenge during research was trying to find a dataset that included both player performance metrics and team information. To solve this issue, we collected data from two APIs and then combined it into one database with multiple tables. Our database included various player performance metrics such as FG%, defensive rebounds (DRB), and personal fouls (PFs).

After collecting the data, we cleaned the data and ran multiple statistical tests on our data to assess our hypotheses.

Findings

Hypothesis 1: Accepted
Strong positive correlation found between plus-minus scores and FG%

  • Interpretation: We think that this is the case because if someone has a higher field goal percentage, they are likely to score more points which would make their plus-minus score higher

Hypothesis 2: Accepted
No significant difference found in players' FG% in March vs entire season

  • Interpretation: We theorize this may be the case due to continuous training, coaching consistency, and player experience

Hypothesis 3: Rejected
Positive correlation found between DRB and PF

  • Interpretation: We think this link exists because players who are more aggressive on defense are more likely to get rebounds and more likely to commit fouls

Conclusion

This project deepened my understanding of how complex and nuanced data analysis can be. For example, the correlation between plus-minus scores and FG% was lower than expected, revealing that many other factors, such as steals and rebounds, influence player performance metrics. I also learned that even with straightforward datasets, it's important to avoid overgeneralizing trends. Overall, this project reinforced the importance of mindful and cautious analysis as I build AI and ML models in order to accurately reflect the real-world data.
