DraftBears Sportsbook

Overview

This project analyzed NBA player performance metrics to uncover trends and correlations that can improve sports analytics. By examining data from different times of the season, we provide insights into how player performance impacts team success.

Context

This project was created by Melvin He, Jos√© Guallpa, and I as a final project for the course CSCI 1951A: Data Science at Brown University in Spring 2024. We were inspired to do this project because we were all fans of the NBA and we wanted to learn how to use data science in sports analytics.

Research Process

A major challenge was trying to find a dataset that included both player performance metrics and team information. My team and I spent a lot of time finding datasets that had one or both of these features. We ultimately collected 2 datasets from multiple APIs and then combined them into one database that included all of the player data we needed. Our database includes various player performance indicators such as points per game, field goal percentage (FGP), defensive rebounds, and personal fouls.

Findings

1. Players with higher plus-minus scores tend to have higher FGP

  • Plus-minus score: difference between points scored by player's team and opposing team while the player on court

2. No significant difference in players' FGP in March vs entire season

3. Players with higher defensive rebounds tend to have higher personal fouls

  • Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, NBA record-holder for most personal fouls and 3rd in rebounds per game

Takeaways

One takeaway from this project is that there are many factors that affect player performance metrics. For example, the correlation between plus-minus scores and field goal percentage is lower than expected. This indicates that there are other factors that affect player performance metrics, such as steals and offensive rebounds. Another takeaway is that even in the simplistic data analysis, one should be careful about overgeneralizing the trends found in the data.

Ethical Considerations

My last takeaway from this is to consider the ethical implications of data analysis. For instance, racial bias is a significant issue in sports analytics. Research by Foy and Roy (2023) found lighter-skinned players were often described for performance and mental abilities, while darker-skinned players were described for physical abilities. Although our datasets lacked qualitative descriptions, we mitigated bias by avoiding analysis linking physical attributes to race.
